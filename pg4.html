4.4 NEGATIVE IMPACTS RURAL LIVELIHOODS ON TISSOH'S WELLBEING AND RURAL LIVLIHOODS

Findings
Based on research data it was evident that less fertile agricultural land has been left behind were 'slash and burn' agriculture has persistentienly occured.
There is widespread high cost of agricultural produce , which is due to decreasing farm output which also in turn is due to decrease in total agricultural labour available in Tissoh Community:
during the study it was found that most able bodied men have left Tissoh Community in seacrh for motorcycle,wage labour jobs in the nearby Kenema City .
And few people especially women and elderly engage in subsistent farming and market gardeninig in Tissoh.



Malaria is also rampant, which may be as a results of the open pits that are now pools of stagnant water:the perfect mosquito breeding site
Extensive gully erosion and topsoil cover damage was observered by the researcher especially were rock mining,charcoal burning and lumbering are prevalent.
Scarcity of once readily available raw material such as sticks for construcrions and medicinal herbs is evident during the course of the study.
