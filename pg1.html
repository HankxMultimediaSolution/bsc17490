the department was mandated with the responsibility of setting up forest ordinances;protected areas, forest reserve,game–reserves,national parks, game parks and wildlife sanctuaries in order to protect wildlife and plant species(Forestry Division Archives).

The 1972 wildlife conservation Act of Sierra Leone governed "the exploitation of wildlife and provided legal protection rights for those endangered species". ( )

The Forest Act 1988 reinforces the Wildlife Conservation Act and focuses on the utility and management of forest resources and allowed for the participation of local communities in forest resources management( Mamoud Jah 2011) .

Moreover Sierra Leone is a signatory to the international Conservation of Biological Diversity and the Ramsar Conservation ( Sierra Leone Conservation and Wildlife Policy, 2010). Therefore so far it can be safely concluded that there exists both locally and internationally the legal framework and institutions for protected area management and conservation . The only aspect lacking is the proper implementation and follow_up on such policies (EPA-SL Report, 2020)

AIMS AND OBJECTIVES

The main AIM of this study :
Is to investigate the negative impacts of rural livelihood activities on the ecological equilibria on protected areas ,
specifically Kambui Hills Forest Reserve and how in turn the anthropogenically modified environment
impacts rural livelihood activities and wellfare ,
whilst examining the inter-linkages and inter-relationships between
rural livelihood activities with respect to PAs based upon "The Sustainable Livelihood Approach Framework" (DFID 1999).

SPECIFIC OBJECTIVES:

  1. Examine the socio-economic and demographic status of the respondents,influencing the natural integrity of the aforementioned protected area.
  2. Examine the rural livelihood activities that disrupts this ecological balance and harmony.
  3. Investigate how the damaged ecosystem of the Kambui Hills Forest Reserve affects the wellbeing of the rural people.
  4. Show interlinkages and relationships between rural livelihood activities and the ecological status and integrity of
    The Kambui Hills Forest Reserve based on 'The Sustainable Livelihood Approach Framework (DFID 1998.
