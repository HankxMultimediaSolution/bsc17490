CHAPTER THREE

METHODOLOGY



3.0
INTRODUCTION

This chapter describes the methods,techniques,and tools used to collect,analyze and present the research data.

3.1


A Case Study design was used ; to examine the Rural livelihood activities undertaken by the adults in Tissoh Community, in the Kambui Hills Forest Reserve.

3.2
Description of Study Area



Landsat and Topographic Maps showing Study Area

This research was conducted in the Kambui Hills Forest Reserve (KHFRN) - North Coordinates x :7deg 51'34" y : 11Deg19'27"
The Kambui Hills Forest Reserve occupies an area of 14,335 hectares in the Eastern Province of Sierra Leone.
It is located 10 kilometres from Kenema City with terrain consisting of steep slopes that reach an altitude of between 100 and 645 metres.
The area mainly contains forest habitat but there is also some savanna and wetland.

3.2.1
RELIEF



The KHFR is part of the interior hills and plataeux regions of Sierra Leone is characterized by ancient crystalline rocks undulating and welll drained.
The terrain of KHFR is a heterogenous landscape of rolling hills with rugged relief and isolated rock outcrops dissected by a series of watercourses and seasonally dry valleys(Gwyne Jones et al)

3.2.2
CLIMATE



The study area experiences a tropical climate and comprises of two main seasons -dry and rainy seasons respectively.
The rainy season are longer from May -November with mean annual rainfall of over 500 cm,compared to other areas with a short dry season.

3.3
SAMPLING

Sample the sample frame for this study comprises of all adults engaged in rural livelihood activities that is a total of 418 persons.
A sample size of twenty percent was selected from the sample size ,that is a total of 70 adults were interviewed using recording schedule based on a semi structutred questionaired developed for the purpose of the study.


3.4
RESEARCH INSTRUMENTS


The major research insttuments usesd in aquring primary data were semi structured recording schedule,focus groups discussions,google forms and semi-structured questionaire.

3.5
DATA COLLECTION PROCEDURES

I. Semi Structured Questionaire :


Semi structure questionaire administration, one questionaire was administered per respondent.Answers to close ended questions were recorded by a tick to the correct answer while the other questions the researcher search further for clarity of what the respondent might mean.
An interpreter was used as the author was not fluent in speaking Mende and also many elders preferred using Mende as their preferred medium of communication.

ii. Focus group discussions


Were designed to discus specific topics such as : local livliehood activities,local demographics,community forest management etc.

3.6
DATA ANALYSIS



3.6.1
CODING OF DATA


After the data was collected I made frequecncy tallies of various responses provided in the questionaire by the respondents.
A pre-coding schedule was done by assigning numbers to close-ended questions, the YES or NO responses only closed values " 1" and "2" respectively.
The codes were later transfered unto a spreadsheet into a table.
3.6.2 SOURCES OF DATA

Both primary and secondary data were collected in a bid to extract information on rural livelihood and protected areas forest conservation management.
The primary data for the research was obtained through personal interviews,semistructured questionaire,personal observations.Whilst secondary
data was obtained from relevant published materials.

STATISTICAL APPROACHES USED

Frequency distribution ,Pie charts ,Histograms .Standard and mean devaition were employed to analyse the collected primary data.
The author also used MeteChart .com an online API for data visualization.
